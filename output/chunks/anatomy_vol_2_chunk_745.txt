Finally, thyroid hormones increase the body’s sensitivity to catecholamines (epinephrine
and norepinephrine) from the adrenal medulla by upregulation of receptors in the blood vessels. When levels of T3 and
T4 hormones are excessive, this effect accelerates the heart rate, strengthens the heartbeat, and increases blood pressure.
 Because thyroid hormones regulate metabolism, heat production, protein synthesis, and many other body functions, thyroid
disorders can have severe and widespread consequences.

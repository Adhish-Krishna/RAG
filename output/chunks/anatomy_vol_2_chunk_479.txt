You do not have a conscious perception of having high blood pressure, but that is
an important afferent branch of the cardiovascular and, particularly, vasomotor reflexes. The sensory neuron is essentially
the same as any other general sensory neuron. The baroreceptor apparatus is part of the ending of a unipolar neuron that has
a cell body in a sensory ganglion. The baroreceptors from the carotid arteries have axons in the glossopharyngeal nerve, and
those from the aorta have axons in the vagus nerve.

Though visceral senses are not primarily a part of conscious perception, those sensations sometimes make it to
conscious awareness. If a visceral sense is strong enough, it will be perceived. The sensory homunculus—the representation
of the body in the primary somatosensory cortex—only has a small region allotted for the perception of internal stimuli.

Bronchus-associated lymphoid tissue (BALT) consists of lymphoid follicular structures with an overlying epithelial
layer found along the bifurcations of the bronchi, and between bronchi and arteries. They also have the typically less-
organized structure of other lymphoid nodules. These tissues, in addition to the tonsils, are effective against inhaled
pathogens.

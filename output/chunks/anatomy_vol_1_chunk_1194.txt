The two-headed biceps brachii crosses the
shoulder and elbow joints to flex the forearm, also taking part in supinating the forearm at the radioulnar joints and flexing
the arm at the shoulder joint. Deep to the biceps brachii, the brachialis provides additional power in flexing the forearm.
 Finally, the brachioradialis can flex the forearm quickly or help lift a load slowly. These muscles and their associated blood
vessels and nerves form the anterior compartment of the arm (anterior flexor compartment of the arm) (Figure 11.25 and
Figure 11.26).

The underlying cause of some neurodegenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s, appears to be related
to proteins—specifically, to proteins behaving badly. One of the strongest theories of what causes Alzheimer’s disease
is based on the accumulation of beta-amyloid plaques, dense conglomerations of a protein that is not functioning
correctly.
